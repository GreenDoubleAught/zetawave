Some other author who is better versed in genetics can tackle this variation. What the Zeta chromosome actually _does_ apart from change biological sex is up to the player. The intention is to permit the player to play through each of the scenarios and keep track of the player's decisions. The changes wrought by the Zeta chromosome and the way these affect society depend on what the player chooses. THE CHOICES There are five basic axes along which the player's choices matter. Major decisions from each story are added to the saved calculations, which persist across multiple playthroughs. (The player will have the option to wipe the history and start fresh.) * Sex. Does the Zeta chromosome only affect AFAB or AMAB? If the player always selects "male" to start, then Zeta becomes a condition only of children born and identified as male at birth. The choice of starting character could become constrained to that single sex. If the player chooses a mixture of male and female starting points, the game will allow both AMAB and AFAB characters to be Zetas. * Social. Does society accept or reject these changes? If the player is more open, more forthright, more bold, and more accepting of the changes, society will shift in that direction. Zetas will get more good publicity as role models emerge on early radio and newsreels, and later in the golden age of television. If the player is more secretive or shameful about these changes, and pushes less against the grain, then society will have a less-good view of Zetas; and Zetas of the future are more likely to conceal their natures or face consequences. The world thus becomes the kind of place that the player creates. * Orientation. Does the Zeta chromosome change your orientation or sex drive? If the player chooses to engage in new sexual behaviors (for instance, going from hetero male to hetero female), then at least sometimes the Zeta chromosome makes this happen. If the player chooses to remain with one sexuality (eg, hetero male to lesbian female) then the Zeta chromosome is _not_ known for changing orientation. The game will keep track of how often this happens and make assumptions about later characters' sexuality. * Mental. Does the Zeta chromosome change the way people think? Is the brain itself altered, setting aside matters of orientation? Do Zetas think differently in each state? * Procreation. Since a Zeta always changes during sex, it makes normal methods of proliferation for the Z chromosome impossible. Therefore, one of two methods must be favored. Either the chromosome is _transmissible_ (you can catch Zeta from somewhere), or the chromosome is _lockable_ (your form can be fixed in one state somehow, either temporarily or permanently). All of the above contribute to the kind of world the player creates. If Zetas are looked down upon, and the state is Lockable, then Zetas may try to fix themselves in a favored shape. If Zetas are disfavored and the condition can be caught, then the condition might be inflicted on others out of spite, and transgender people may secretly aspire to catch the condition. And so on.
